Acoustic-based Dietary Data Collection

An automated, non-intrusive tool for objective dietary habit analysis.

Addressing the Challenge of Manual Dietary Recall

Manual dietary logs and retrospective recall methods are often subject to human error, bias, and incomplete data. This poses a significant challenge for researchers and individuals seeking accurate insights into eating patterns.

Our application offers a novel solution: passive, objective data capture through acoustic event recognition.

Methodology

The application employs a sophisticated machine learning model to continuously analyze ambient sound for pre-defined kitchen-related acoustic events. When a cluster of these events is detected, the system initiates and timestamps a "meal session."

This process operates strictly on-device, ensuring no raw audio data is ever transmitted or stored, thus maintaining participant privacy and data integrity.

Core Features

Passive Data Acquisition

Eliminates the need for manual logging, capturing data effortlessly in the background.

Privacy-Preserving Architecture

All sound analysis is performed locally on the device; no audio data leaves the phone.

Objective Time-stamped Records

Provides a precise, objective timeline of eating events, valuable for research and personal insights.
